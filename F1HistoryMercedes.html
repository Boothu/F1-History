Mercedes AMG Petronas

1954-Present

Mercedes-Benz, a German automaker, has a storied history in Formula 1. Here's a concise overview:

Formation and Early Years (1954-1955)

  • Mercedes-Benz made its Formula 1 debut in 1954 as a factory team with drivers Juan Manuel Fangio and Karl Kling.
  • In their inaugural season, Fangio won the Drivers' Championship, and Mercedes-Benz secured the Constructors' Championship.

Withdrawal (1956-2000)

  • Mercedes-Benz withdrew from Formula 1 after the 1955 season due to a tragic accident at the 1955 Le Mans race.
  • They focused on other motorsport endeavors before making a partial return as an engine supplier in the 1990s.

Return and Golden Years (2010-2019)

  • In 2010, Mercedes-Benz returned as a factory team, rebranded as Mercedes Grand Prix (later Mercedes-AMG Petronas Formula One Team).
  • They struggled initially but made progress, especially after signing Lewis Hamilton in 2013.
  • Mercedes dominated the hybrid era, winning 7 Drivers' and 8 Constructors' Championships.

The End of Dominance (2020-Present)

  • Mercedes-AMG Petronas continued its success in the 2020 Season with Lewis Hamilton and Valtteri Bottas as drivers.
  • Their 7 year reign came to a halt when Max Verstappen won the 2021 WDC in a controversial Abu Dhabi GP, although Mercedes still won the WCC this year by a thin margin.
  • Mercedes fully slipped out of prominence by the 2022 season, having only the 3rd fastest car, although in 2023 they are putting a fight to Ferrari for 2nd fastest.

Throughout its history, Mercedes-Benz has been synonymous with engineering excellence and dominance in Formula 1. Their silver livery is iconic in the sport, and their achievements have solidified their position as one of the most successful teams in Formula 1 history.
